# 5. AI Creators
Practical advice for data scientists and machine learning engineers with regard to the intellectual property (IP) regimes they work within.

## 5.1 What to look for: Hackathons
The use of data science is often incentivised in competitions, referred to as hackathons. Two things to look for when reviewing hacksthon (or other data science competitions) terms and conditions.

### The 'I keep all' IP clause
"All IP produced during the hackathon will belong to the participants."

### The 'I own it but you can use it for free' IP Clause
"To the extent of your ownership of intellectual property rights (IP Rights) in the entry, grant [_organising committee entity name here_] a royalty free, irrevocable, perpetual, non-exclusive, transferable licence which, at a minimum, permits [_organising committee entity name here_] to use, copy, translate, reproduce, adapt and in any way exploit the IP Rights in such entry."


## 5.2 What to negotiate: Workplace agreements
Data scientists skills are in demand and to the extent they work on their on projects outside of work, including hackathons, an understanding of the terms of their contract of employment is required. Typically, employers own the IP of what has been produced under contracts of employment. As a result, it is recommended data scientists negotiate and IP clause in their employment contract that perist them to produce their own IP, when they are not at work. In any event, data science best practice involves keeping things separate: do not work on your own project during work hours nor on a work coputer.
